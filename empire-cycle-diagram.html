Empire Comparison — Glubb's Five Stages Across History
KEY FINDING

The 250-year rule is an average with a 4× spread, not a law. Lifespans in this set range from 162 years (Mongol) to 623 years (Ottoman). America at ~250 years sits at the statistical mean — but the mean predicts nothing about any individual case.

OTTOMAN EXCEPTION

The Ottoman Empire stayed in Stage 4 for ~170 years through repeated military and administrative reform (Tanzimat, 1839). Prolonged Stage 4 is historically possible — but in every case here, Stage 5 eventually followed regardless of reform attempts.

NO FIXED TIMELINE

Stage 5 speed varies enormously: the British Empire collapsed from Stage 4 to dissolution in ~10 years (1939–1949). The Roman Empire spent ~241 years in Stage 5. America has been in Stage 4 for ~25 years. Historical precedent gives no reliable estimate for how long Stage 4 can last — only that no empire in this set reversed it.

Stage 5 Trigger — Three Forces Converging
💣
War It Cannot Win
Military-industrial prisoner's dilemma prevents withdrawal. Each escalation drains resources and credibility while the debt spiral deepens.
🌀
Debt It Cannot Repay
$36T debt + $1T annual interest + war costs. Mathematical spiral with only two exits — default or inflation — both destroying the currency.
🔄
Currency Being Replaced
Multiple independent actors — China, Russia, Saudi Arabia, India, Brazil — each rationally moving away from the dollar. No coordination needed. Dominant strategy convergence.
Three forces do not require coordination or conspiracy ↓ They simply require Stage 4 conditions to persist long enough ↓ Then they converge → Stage 5

They saw each problem individually. They did not see the convergence until it was complete.
