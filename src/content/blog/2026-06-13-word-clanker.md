## Clanker /ˈklaŋkər/

**Part of speech:** noun (also attributive)

**Etymology:** English, from *clank* (a sharp metallic sound, imitative in origin, attested c. 1650) + the agentive suffix *-er*. Popularized as a pejorative for mechanical beings in the animated series *Star Wars: The Clone Wars* (2008), where clone troopers hurled it at battle droids. Adopted into mainstream internet slang c. 2024–2025.

---

### Definitions

1. *(primary meaning)* A derogatory term for a robot, android, or other autonomous machine, especially one perceived as crude, intrusive, or threatening to human roles.
2. *(extended meaning)* By extension, any artificial-intelligence system or product — chatbots, generative models, automated agents — invoked dismissively.
3. *(informal / colloquial usage)* A jab directed at a person who relies excessively on AI tools, or whose output reads as machine-generated and soulless.

### Usage in a Sentence

> "The drive-through swapped its cashier for a screen, and the woman ahead of me muttered, 'Great, another clanker taking jobs.'"

> "He ran his entire essay through three models and turned in pure clanker prose — competent, frictionless, and completely forgettable."

### Why This Word Is Everywhere Right Now

The rise of *clanker* tracks almost perfectly with the saturation of everyday life by artificial intelligence. As generative models moved from novelty to infrastructure — answering customer-service lines, drafting emails, narrating videos, and increasingly steering "agentic" software that acts on our behalf — people reached for a word to express a very old emotion aimed at a very new target: resentment. Borrowed from a galaxy far, far away, *clanker* gave that resentment a satisfying, percussive shape. It sounds like what it means: something hard, hollow, and mechanical.

What makes the term culturally telling is its ambivalence. It is used by people who fear automation displacing their work, by critics weary of low-effort machine-made "slop," and, just as often, ironically — by the very users who depend on these tools daily. To call a chatbot a clanker is to keep it at arm's length, to assert that the human doing the calling is still the one in charge. Linguists have noted that the word functions as a kind of social boundary marker, a way of sorting the authentically human from the synthetically convenient at a moment when that line is blurring fast.

There is also unease beneath the humor. Commentators have observed that *clanker* arrived pre-loaded as a fictional slur, and that deploying it against real machines rehearses the rhetoric of dehumanization — a quirk that has sparked genuine debate about how we will talk about, and to, intelligent systems as they grow more lifelike. For now, the word remains a release valve: a small, sardonic protest against a world increasingly run by things that go clank.

### Related Words

- **slop** — low-quality, mass-produced AI-generated content; what clankers are accused of churning out.
- **agentic** — describing AI that acts autonomously toward goals, the capability that makes clankers feel less like tools and more like rivals.
- **Luddite** — a 19th-century textile worker who smashed machines; the historical ancestor of today's clanker-basher.
